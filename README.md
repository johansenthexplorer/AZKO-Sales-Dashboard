An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing 8 months (Jan–Aug 2024) of sales transaction data for PT. Aspirasi Hidup Indonesia, Tbk. (AZKO Hardware). The project translates raw transactional data into business insights and actionable recommendations across three layers: overall performance, product-level drivers, and regional/payment behavior.

**Business Questions**

Overall Performance:
1. What was the total online sales revenue during the recorded period?
2. In which month or on which date was the highest revenue generated?
3. Which product category generated the highest revenue?
4. What was the sales trend from January to August 2024?
5. Were there any significant fluctuations in sales performance during the period?

Product-Level Performance:
1. What product generated the highest revenue?
2. Which product category recorded the highest number of units sold?
3. Do high-priced products always generate high revenue?
4. Which product has a high price but low sales volume?
5. What product strategy can be recommended based on the analysis?


Regional & Payment Behavior:
1. Which region generated the highest total revenue?
2. Which region recorded the highest number of units sold?
3. Which payment method is used most frequently?
4. Are certain payment methods more dominant in specific regions?
5. What recommendations can be provided for regional and payment method strategies?


**Tools & Methods**
Power BI: data modeling, DAX measures (Total Revenue, Revenue Average, Price Average, Transaction Total), interactive slicers (region, category, payment method, date range)
Data preparation: cleaning and validating transactional fields (units sold, unit price, revenue, region, payment method, product category, date)
Analysis approach: layered dashboard structure — macro trend → product drivers → regional/payment context — designed so each layer builds on the previous one

**Key Findings**

Overall Performance:
1. Total revenue: Rp6.83 billion from 518 units across 240 transactions
2. Revenue trend (Jan–Aug 2024) is highly volatile — sharp spikes followed by steep declines, most notably in April (spike on Apr 22, sharp drop on Apr 23)

Product-Level:
1. Highest-revenue product: Canon EOS R5 Camera (Rp390K from 1 unit) — premium, low-volume, high-impact
2. Highest sales volume: Clothing category (145 units) high demand, lower margin per unit
3. Electronics generated the highest category revenue (~Rp3.08B), driven by both high price and reasonable volume
4. Price alone doesn't determine revenue volume is a critical secondary driver


Regional & Payment:
1. North America: highest revenue (~Rp3.25B) — most valuable market
2. Asia: highest unit volume (233 units) — strong demand, lower revenue-per-unit
3. Credit Card: most-used payment method overall (120 transactions, 50%)
4. Regional payment preferences differ — North America & Asia favor Credit Card, Europe favors PayPal


**Recommendations**
1. De-risk the revenue base — reduce reliance on sporadic premium spikes (e.g., Canon EOS R5) by converting high-value buyers into a repeatable segment
2. Scale the volume engine — leverage Clothing's strong demand for upselling/cross-selling into adjacent categories
3. Match payment infrastructure to regional behavior — protect Credit Card dominance in North America/Asia, invest in PayPal for Europe
4. Align inventory strategy with the price-volume split — consistent stock for fast-moving categories, careful planning for premium low-frequency items
